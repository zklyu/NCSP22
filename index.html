"Study on Expressiveness of Speech Synthesis Using Multi-resolution Modulation-filtered Cochleagram"


Authors:Kaili,Zhang and Masashi Unoki
Abstract:SEmotion information is a key part of expressive speech synthesis research. At present, researchers mainly extract emotional information from mel-spectrogram, and improve the expressiveness of synthesized speech through emotional information and the improvement of model structure. However, the mel filterbank used to extract mel-spectrogram has serious energy leakage between adjacent frequency bands, which is not conducive to extracting formant features. Therefore, we introduce an auditory feature called multi-resolution modulation-filtered cochleagram (MMCG), which simulates the auditory signal processing mechanism and reflects the function of the human auditory system. In this work, we propose to extract emotional information from MMCG, and design a MMCG encoder according to the characteristics of MMCG, which aims to improve the expressiveness of synthesized speech. Both subjective and objective experiments demonstrate that our proposed method not only improves the naturalness and style transfer performance of synthesized speech, but also reduces the F0 error, compared with traditional method using mel-spectrogram as feature input.
