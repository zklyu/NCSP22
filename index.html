"Study on Expressiveness of Speech Synthesis Using Multi-resolution Modulation-filtered Cochleagram"


Authors:Kaili Zhang and Masashi Unoki
Abstract:Emotion information is a key part of expressive speech synthesis research. At present, researchers mainly extract emotion information from mel-spectrogram, and improve the expressiveness of synthesized speech through emotion information and the improvement of model structure. However, the mel filterbank used to extract mel-spectrogram has serious energy leakage between adjacent frequency bands, which is not conducive to extracting formant features. Therefore, an auditory feature called multi-resolution modulation-filtered cochleagram (MMCG) has been introduced to simulate the auditory signal processing mechanism and to reflect the function of the human auditory system. This paper proposes to extract emotion information from MMCG,and design a MMCG encoder according to the characteristics of MMCG, which aims to improve the expressiveness of synthesized speech. Both subjective and objective experiments demonstrate that our proposed method not only improves the naturalness and style transfer performance of synthesized speech, but also reduces the F0 error, compared with traditional method using mel-spectrogram as feature input.
